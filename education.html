Education

Here's what I've learned!

I began my higher education journey at Brigham Young University in 2020. I decided to study international relations because I wanted to gain both skills and knowledge that I could use to go forth and serve in the world. Through my coursework in the international relations major, I developed skills in policy research, communication, data analysis, and writing. Additionally, I gained a deeper understanding of domestic and foreign politics and a deep respect for different people and cultures.

As I was trying to decide what to do for graduate school, I knew I wanted to continue on my path of developing skills that could help me better serve in the world. My experience in the BYU Master of Public Administration program has helped me immensely along this path. I have further developed my interpersonal communication, qualitative and quantitative data analysis, writing, and presentation skills, while also gaining a greater understanding of the public sector.

I look forward to applying my educational background in public administration, international relations, and economics after graduation.
